COUNSELING BASICS

1. Introduction
Counseling is a professional relationship that empowers individuals to achieve mental wellness, resolve personal challenges, and improve overall functioning. Ethical counseling requires respect, empathy, and confidentiality.

2. Core Principles
- Respect: Treat clients as autonomous individuals, valuing their experiences and perspectives.
- Empathy: Actively understand clients’ emotions and viewpoints without judgment.
- Non-maleficence: Avoid causing harm to clients through actions or advice.
- Beneficence: Promote clients’ well-being and growth.
- Confidentiality: Protect client information unless legal or safety exceptions apply.
- Informed Consent: Ensure clients understand the counseling process, limits, and expectations before beginning.

3. Communication Techniques
- Active Listening: Focus fully on the client, reflect their words, and clarify feelings.
- Open-Ended Questions: Encourage exploration of thoughts and emotions.
- Summarizing: Help clients organize their thoughts and recognize patterns.
- Nonverbal Cues: Maintain attentive body language and appropriate facial expressions.

4. Counseling Process
- Intake: Collect background, presenting concerns, and client expectations.
- Goal Setting: Collaboratively define realistic, measurable objectives.
- Intervention: Apply ethical therapeutic techniques tailored to client needs.
- Evaluation: Monitor progress and adjust methods accordingly.
- Termination: End sessions thoughtfully, reviewing progress and future steps.

5. Ethical Considerations
- Avoid Dual Relationships: Prevent conflicts of interest with clients.
- Cultural Sensitivity: Respect diversity and individual values.
- Record Keeping: Maintain accurate and secure client records.
- Self-Reflection: Monitor personal biases and emotional reactions that may affect counseling.
